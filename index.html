Chenglei Si

Hi! I am an undergraduate (CS + Linguistics) at the University of Maryland where I am advised by Prof. Jordan Boyd-Graber. I started doing research in NLP during high school thanks to the guidance of Prof. Min-Yen Kan. I spent my freshman year visiting THUNLP working with Prof. Zhiyuan Liu. I also collaborate with many other friends from Microsoft, NYU, Princeton, UW, and UChicago.

My research interests evolve over time but generally they relate to: (1) Reliability and Generalizability: How to defend against adversarial attacks? How to calibrate model confidence to inform user decision making? When can models combat spurious correlation and input noises? How does the rising prompting paradigm compare to supervised models in terms of reliability? (2) Question Answering: How do we properly evaluate the performance of QA models? On what cases our QA models would succeed or fail, and why? (3) Language Representation: What is the optimal granularity for language representation? Can we design more efficient and robust language models by leveraging linguistic insights?
